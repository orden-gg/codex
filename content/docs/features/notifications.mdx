Fireball supports **push notifications** to keep you informed about job completions, service updates, and other events — even when you're not on the site.

## Setting Up Notifications

1. Go to **Settings** in the dashboard sidebar
2. Find the **Notification Settings** section
3. Click **Enable Notifications**
4. Allow browser notification permissions when prompted
5. Select which notification types you want to receive
6. Click **Save & Enable**

<Callout type="warning">
  If your browser has blocked notifications for Fireball, you'll need to manually re-enable them in
  your browser settings. Look for the lock icon in your address bar to manage site permissions.
</Callout>

## Managing Notifications

Once enabled, you can customize your notifications:

- **All Notifications** — Master checkbox to enable or disable everything
- **Per-type toggles** — Enable or disable individual notification types
- **Save Changes** — Apply your updated preferences
- **Disable All** — Turn off notifications entirely

## Push Notifications vs. Browser

Push notifications work through your browser's notification system:

- **Desktop** — Appears as system notifications
- **Mobile PWA** — Appears as native push notifications (requires the app to be installed)
- **Background delivery** — Notifications arrive even when the Fireball tab is closed

## Requirements

- A modern browser that supports the Push API
- Browser notification permission granted for Fireball
- Service Worker registered (happens automatically)

## App Updates

Fireball's service worker checks for updates every 60 minutes. When a new version is available, you'll be prompted to refresh. This ensures you always have the latest notification capabilities.
